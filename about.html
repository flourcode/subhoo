About Subhoo

What is This?!

Subhoo shows you the hidden partnerships behind federal prime and subcontractor relationships—so you can follow the money, find teaming partners, or figure out who your competitors are working with. It's built for federal sales and BD teams who’ve ever been asked, “Who’s the sub?” or “Who else should we be talking to?”

I built Subhoo because I got tired of stitching together intel from SAM.gov, USAspending, and a dozen browser tabs just to understand one deal. Now it’s all in one place. You pick an agency, and Subhoo visualizes the actual subcontracting activity reported through USAspending.gov: which office is spending, who they’re awarding to, and which subs are on the team.

If you’re only talking to one contact at one prime, you’re probably single-threaded. That means your deal can vanish with a reorg, a re-compete, or someone forgetting to CC you on the strategy call. Subhoo helps you map the full picture—starting with which Agency and Office is spending the money, who they’re awarding it to, and which subs are already supporting the work.

This kind of visibility lets you form stronger joint opportunities and avoid blind spots in your account plans. As Jeb Blount says in Fanatical Prospecting, “The number one reason for empty pipelines is the failure to consistently prospect.” Subhoo gives you a smarter starting point—so you’re not just working harder, but aiming better too.

Where the Data Comes From

All data in Subhoo is sourced directly from USAspending.gov. It’s 100% public. I just make it visual and easier to use. Files are refreshed monthly.

Subhoo includes all reported prime-to-subcontractor relationships above federal reporting thresholds. Some companies may not appear due to underreporting or data gaps—but if it’s been filed, it’s probably in here.
