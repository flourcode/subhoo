How to Use Subhoo

A Guide for Sales & BD Prospecting

Subhoo visually maps the hidden partnerships between Federal Primes and Subcontractors using public data from USAspending.gov. For Sales & BD pros, this means you can quickly identify potential teaming partners, analyze competitor relationships, follow the money from agency offices to subs, target your outreach, and go into meetings prepared.

Hereâ€™s a step-by-step guide to leveraging Subhoo for pipeline generation:

Step 1: Select Your Target Agency

Use the Select agency dropdown menu in the sidebar to focus your analysis on a specific agency (e.g., Army, HHS, DHS) where you are pursuing opportunities.

Step 2: Choose Your Initial View

Click the buttons under Chart Type in the sidebar:

  • Contract Flow (Sankey): Default view. Great for seeing the overall flow of money and identifying the biggest players (nodes) and funding flows (links).
  • Network: Shows relationships as connected circles. Useful for spotting clusters of activity and influential connectors. Node size often relates to contract value.
  • Map: Visualizes subcontract value by Place of Performance state. Helpful for geographic targeting.

Step 3: Understand the Big Picture (Stats Cards)

Review the four cards below the header. They show the total filtered subcontract value, top Primes awarding subs, top Subs receiving awards, and the primary NAICS code distribution for your current view.

Step 4: Refine Your Focus (Filters)

Use the sidebar filters to narrow down the data:

  • Visible Nodes: Under "Display options", toggle buttons (Agency, Dept, Office, Prime, Sub) to hide/show levels in the Sankey/Network charts.
  • Min Contract Value: Use the slider under "Display options" to filter out smaller subcontracts and focus on higher-value relationships.
  • Select agency / Office: Use the second dropdown under "Select agency" to drill down into a specific funding Office within the selected Agency.
  • Optional Filters: Filter by a specific Prime Contractor (shows only their subs), or by NAICS Code (shows only subcontracts where the Prime contract matches that industry).
  • Search: Use the keyword search bar to find specific Primes, Subs, or NAICS keywords within the current agency's data.

Step 5: Dive Deep into Relationships

Interact directly with the visualizations:

  • Hover: Move your mouse over nodes and links (Sankey/Network) or states (Map) to see tooltips with names and values.
  • Click Nodes/States: Click on an element (Agency, Office, Prime, Sub, or State) to focus the view and data tables on just that item and its direct connections. Use the "All Data" breadcrumb link (that appears above the stats cards) to reset the focus.
  • Info Panel: Clicking often opens a panel on the right with more details and links to USAspending.gov.

Step 6: Analyze Detailed Data (Tables)

Scroll down below the visualization:

  • Detailed Subaward Data Table: Shows the filtered subaward details. Click column headers to sort. Click the "View" link to go directly to the contract on USAspending.gov.
  • Analysis Tables: Show the Top Primes, Top Subs, and Top NAICS Codes based on the entire dataset for the selected agency (not affected by the value slider).

Step 7: Identify Timely Opportunities (Expiring Contracts)

Check the Expiring Contracts (> $200K ~6 Mo) table. It lists contracts where the Prime's potential end date is near, highlighting potential re-competes or needs for new subcontractors. Sort by date, load more results, and use the links to verify on USAspending.gov.

Step 8: Export Data

Click the Download... CSV buttons above the main data table or the expiring contracts table to download the currently filtered data for offline use or CRM import.

Step 9: Reset & Explore More

Click the Reset all filters button at the bottom of the sidebar to clear all selections and start a new search.

Step 10: Theme & Sharing

Use the sun/moon icon in the header to toggle light/dark mode. Use the share icon to send the current page link to others.
